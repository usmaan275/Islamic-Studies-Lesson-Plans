# 5:30–5:40 | Preservation During the Prophet’s ﷺ Time  
(Book Page 52)

---

## How Was the Qurʾān Preserved?

Explain:

The Qurʾān was preserved in **two main ways**:

### 1 — Memorisation

Many companions memorised the Qurʾān.

Not just parts —
but the entire Qurʾān.

These memorizers were called:

**Ḥuffāẓ**

Some companions memorised:
- Immediately after revelation
- Directly from the Prophet ﷺ

---

### 2 — Writing

The Qurʾān was also written down.

On:
- Leather
- Bone
- Stone
- Palm leaves

The Prophet ﷺ had official scribes.

Whenever revelation came:
He instructed them exactly where to place verses.

Nothing was random.

Everything was organised.

---

# The Seven Aḥruf

Explain simply:

The Qurʾān was revealed in **seven aḥruf**.

Meaning:

Different dialects
for different Arab tribes.

Why?

Because:

Arabia had many dialects.

Not everyone spoke the same way.

Allah made the Qurʾān easier for people to recite.

---

## Teacher Clarification

Explain:

This did NOT change the message.

Only pronunciation differences.

The meaning remained the same.

# 5:40–5:50 | Collection Under Abū Bakr رضي الله عنه  
(Book Page 53)

---

## Why Was the Qurʾān Collected?

After the death of the Prophet ﷺ,
many memorizers were killed in battle.

Especially during:

**Battle of Yamāmah**

ʿUmar رضي الله عنه feared:

If more memorizers died,
some Qurʾān might be lost.

So he suggested:

Collect the Qurʾān into one written book.

---

## Who Was Assigned This Task?

**Zayd ibn Thābit رضي الله عنه**

He was:
- A scribe of revelation
- Trustworthy
- Intelligent
- Known for accuracy

---

## Strict Collection Method

Explain carefully:

They did NOT just write from memory.

They required:

- Written proof
- Multiple witnesses
- Verified memorisation

Nothing was accepted unless verified.

This produced:

**The first official compiled manuscript.**

---

# Where Was This Copy Kept?

It was passed to:

1 — Abū Bakr رضي الله عنه  
Then to:

2 — ʿUmar رضي الله عنه  
Then to:

3 — Ḥafṣah رضي الله عنها  

Before reaching:

**ʿUthmān رضي الله عنه**

---

# 5:50–6:00 | Standardisation Under ʿUthmān رضي الله عنه  
(Book Page 55)

---

## Why Was Standardisation Needed?

Islam spread far beyond Arabia.

New Muslims spoke:
- Different languages
- Different dialects

Some disagreements began in recitation.

Not corruption —
but confusion.

---

## What Did ʿUthmān رضي الله عنه Do?

He ordered:

- Official copies made
- Based on Ḥafṣah’s manuscript
- Verified by companions

These copies were sent to:

Major cities across the empire.

---

## Why Were Other Manuscripts Burned?

Explain clearly:

Not to destroy Qurʾān —
but to prevent confusion.

Some personal notes included:

- Explanations
- Commentary
- Dialect notes

To avoid disagreement:

Only the official version remained.

This preserved unity.

---

# Teacher Emphasis

Without this step:

Different versions might have appeared.

But because of this:

**The Qurʾān remained one.**

---

# Reflection 1 — Importance of Tajwīd

Explain:

The Qurʾān is not just read.

It is recited.

With correct pronunciation.

This is called:

**Tajwīd**

Why?

Because even small pronunciation changes
can affect meaning.

---

# Reflection 2 — Role of Ḥuffāẓ

Today:

Millions of people have memorised the Qurʾān.

Children.
Adults.
Elderly.

Across:

- Different countries
- Different languages
- Different cultures

Yet:

All recite the same Qurʾān.

Word for word.

---

# Reflection 3 — Qurʾān as a Living Miracle

Most books:

Are read.

The Qurʾān:

Is memorised.
Recited.
Studied.
Lived.

Every day.

For over 1400 years.

# 5:10–5:15 | Introduction to ʿĀlam al-Ghayb  
(Page 59)

---

## What is al-Ghayb?

Explain clearly:

Al-Ghayb means:

**That which we cannot see, hear, or perceive with our senses.**

Examples:
- Allah
- Angels
- Jinn
- The future
- The afterlife

---

## Teacher Explanation

Say:

Just because we cannot see something —
does not mean it doesn’t exist.

Ask:

> Can you see WiFi?

No.

But you know it exists because of its effects.

Likewise:

We know the unseen exists
because Allah informed us.

---

# Section 1 — Belief in Allah

---

## Key Point

Allah is:
- Not seen
- Not limited
- Not like creation

Yet:

He is closer to us than anything.

---

## Teacher Line

Say:

You cannot see Him.

But everything you see —
points to Him.

# 5:30–5:40 | Angels — al-Malāʾ al-Asfal  
(Page 60 Half)

---

## What Are Angels?

Created from:
- Light

They:
- Do NOT disobey Allah
- Have NO desires
- Carry out commands perfectly

---

## Roles of Angels

### 1 — Recording Deeds

Two angels with every person:
- One writes good
- One writes bad

Ask:

> Imagine if everything you did was being recorded…

(Then say:)

It is.

---

### 2 — Loving Those Allah Loves

Explain:

Angels love:
- The righteous
- The obedient
- Those who remember Allah

---

## Teacher Reflection

Say:

If angels are around you right now…

What kind of environment are you giving them?

# 5:40–5:45 | al-Malāʾ al-Aʿlā (Higher Angels)  
(Page 60–61)

---

## Higher Angels

Explain:

Some angels have higher status.

Examples:
- Jibrīl (revelation)
- Mikāʾīl (provision)
- Isrāfīl (trumpet)

---

## Teacher Emphasis

These are not symbolic.

They are real.

They have roles.

And they are part of Allah’s system.

---

# 5:45–5:55 | Jinn — History & Reality  
(Page 61)

---

## What Are Jinn?

Created from:
- Smokeless fire

They:
- Have free will
- Can choose good or evil

---

## Before the Qurʾān

Explain:

Jinn used to:
- Climb the skies
- Listen to conversations of angels
- Pass information to fortune tellers

---

## After Revelation

This was stopped.

Now:
- They try
- But are blocked
- Information is incomplete and unreliable

---

## Story from Sūrah al-Jinn

Explain simply:

A group of jinn heard the Qurʾān.

They said:

"This is something amazing."

They accepted Islam.

---

## Teacher Reflection

Say:

Even the jinn heard the Qurʾān…
and changed.

What about us?

# 5:55–6:00 | False Claims to the Unseen  
(Page 62 Half)

---

## Clear Teaching

Say clearly:

NO ONE knows the unseen except Allah.

---

## Examples to Give Students

- Horoscopes
- Star signs
- Tarot cards
- Palm reading

---

## Teacher Explanation (Strong Tone)

Say:

These are not just "fun".

They are dangerous.

Because:

They claim knowledge that belongs only to Allah.

---

## Real-Life Example

Say:

Has anyone ever predicted:
- Exactly what will happen tomorrow?
- Every event in their life?

No.

Because:

The future belongs to Allah alone.

# 6:00–6:07 | Evil Eye (al-ʿAyn) & Barakah  
(Page 62–63)

---

## What is al-ʿAyn?

Explain:

The evil eye is when:
- Someone looks at something
- With jealousy or admiration
- Without mentioning Allah

And harm can occur.

---

## Protection

Teach students:

Always say:

**"Mā shāʾ Allāh"**  
**"BārakAllāhu fīh"**

Make duʿā for protection.

---

## Barakah vs Loss

Explain clearly:

Barakah means:

**More benefit than expected.**

---

## Stories of Barakah

- ʿĀʾishah رضي الله عنها and food increasing  
- Companions having food that didn’t finish  
- :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} feeding many from little  

---

## About the “Scientific Idea”

Explain carefully (important):

Some people say:

Observation affects outcomes.

In modern science (like quantum physics),
there are cases where observing something
changes how it behaves.

BUT:

We do NOT base belief in ʿayn on science.

We believe it because:
**The Prophet ﷺ taught it.**

Science may hint at things —
but revelation gives certainty.

---

## Teacher Line

Say:

Barakah increases things.

ʿAyn reduces things.

Both happen by the will of Allah.

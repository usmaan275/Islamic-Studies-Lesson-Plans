## Read Pages 66–67

# Key Concepts

## What is ʿAdl?

ʿAdl means:

- Perfect justice
- Perfect fairness
- Putting everything in its correct place
- Giving everyone exactly what they deserve

Allah says:

> “Indeed Allah does not wrong even the weight of an atom.”
(Surah al-Nisāʾ 4:40)

Explain:

- Human judges make mistakes
- Humans are emotional
- Humans forget
- Humans can be bribed
- Humans do not know the full story

But Allah:

- Knows every detail
- Knows every hidden intention
- Knows every pain
- Knows every sacrifice
- Knows every injustice
- Never forgets anything

---

# Teacher Reflection

Tell students:

Sometimes in this life it feels like:

- bad people get away with things
- good people suffer
- innocent people are oppressed

But Islam teaches us:

> This world is not the final courtroom.

The real justice comes on Yawm al-Qiyāmah.

---

# Powerful Example

Ask students:

- Have you ever been blamed for something you didn’t do?
- Has someone ever lied about you?
- Have you ever seen unfairness in school or online?

Then say:

> Imagine a Day where every hidden truth comes out.  
> Every lie exposed.  
> Every injustice corrected.  
> Every oppressed person finally answered.

That is the justice of Allah.

# 5:20 – 5:35 | Justice on the Day of Judgement

## Read Half of Page 68

---

# Hadith of Good Deeds Being Taken

Explain the famous hadith:

A person may come on the Day of Judgement with:

- Salah
- Fasting
- Charity
- Good deeds

But because they:

- hurt people
- insulted others
- oppressed others
- backbit people
- wronged people

their good deeds will be taken and given away.

And if their good deeds finish:

the sins of the oppressed are placed onto them.

---

# Teacher Story – The Man Who Upset His Mother

Mention briefly:

There was a man at the time of the Sahabah who was dying but struggled to say the shahādah.

The reason?

He had deeply upset and hurt his mother.

Only after she forgave him was he able to say:
> Lā ilāha illa Allah.

---

# Reflection

Tell students:

Many people think Islam is only:

- praying
- fasting
- Qurʾān

But Islam also includes:

- character
- kindness
- respecting parents
- respecting people
- not harming others

You can pray all night,
but if you destroy people with your tongue,
that will also be accounted for.

---

# Discussion Question

Ask:

> Which is harder:
>
> worshipping Allah,
> or treating people properly every single day?

Allow brief discussion.

# 5:35 – 5:50 | The Scales of Justice & Human Circumstances

## Read Rest of Page 68 and Page 69

---

# The Scales (Mīzān)

Explain:

On the Day of Judgement deeds will be weighed.

Not all deeds weigh the same.

Sometimes:

- one sincere deed outweighs hundreds
- one small sin destroys years of goodness

Because Allah judges:
- sincerity
- sacrifice
- struggle
- intention
- difficulty

---

# The Hadith of the Two Dirhams

Explain:

A poor man gave two dirhams in charity.

A rich man gave thousands.

But the poor man’s charity was greater in reward because:

- it cost him more
- it was harder for him
- it came from genuine sacrifice

---

# Actions Based on Circumstances

## Surah al-Isrāʾ 17:84

> “Each person acts according to their own shākilah…”

---

# Analysis of the Word “Shākilah”

Explain carefully:

The word “shākilah” means:

- a person’s nature
- circumstances
- background
- environment
- inner state
- life situation

Meaning:

Every person has a different story.

Some people:

- grew up with Islam
- others didn’t

Some people:

- had loving homes
- others had chaos

Some people:

- were taught good manners
- others never were

Some people struggle with:
- anger
- addiction
- trauma
- loneliness
- family issues

Allah knows every detail.

---

# Key Lesson

Tell students strongly:

> Never think you are better than someone else.

Because maybe:

- your test is easier
- your environment was easier
- your temptations are different

And maybe that sinner you look down upon:
- cried one sincere tear to Allah
- and Allah forgave them completely.

---

# Natural Disasters Discussion

Mention briefly:

Students often ask:
> “Why do disasters happen?”

Explain carefully:

Sometimes they are:
- tests
- reminders
- consequences
- means of purification

But we do NOT claim to know exactly why every tragedy happens.

A Muslim responds with:
- compassion
- duʿā
- humility

not arrogance.

---

# Reflection

Say:

The more a person understands Allah’s justice,
the less arrogant they become.

# 5:50 – 6:00 | Oppression & Standing for Justice

## Read Page 70 and Half of 71

---

# Key Theme

Islam is strongly against oppression.

Oppression includes:

- bullying
- racism
- mocking others
- abuse
- cheating
- backbiting
- injustice
- arrogance
- taking rights away from people

---

# Powerful Hadith

Allah says in a Hadith Qudsi:

> “O My servants, I have forbidden oppression upon Myself and made it forbidden between you.”

---

# Teacher Reflection

Tell students:

Sometimes the oppressed person:
- has no power
- no followers
- no money
- no voice

But Allah hears them.

And the duʿā of the oppressed is extremely powerful.

---

# Real-Life Connection

Mention:

People often think:
- popularity = success
- wealth = success
- followers = success

But Allah judges differently.

A person can appear powerful in this world
yet be bankrupt on the Day of Judgement.

# 6:00 – 6:08 | Allah’s Faḍl & Mercy

## Read Rest of Page 71 and Page 72

---

# What is Faḍl?

Faḍl means:
- favour
- generosity
- extra mercy
- blessings beyond what we deserve

If Allah only dealt with us through justice,
none of us would survive.

We rely upon:
- His mercy
- His forgiveness
- His generosity

---

# Reflection on Blessings

Ask students:

- Did you create your eyes?
- Did you choose your family?
- Did you create your heartbeat?
- Did you earn the air you breathe?

Everything is from Allah.

---

# Important Point

A person enters Jannah ultimately through Allah’s mercy.

Even our good deeds are only possible because Allah allowed us to do them.

---

# Powerful Reflection

Tell students:

Sometimes Allah gives us:
- chances after sins
- opportunities after failure
- guidance after darkness

That is His Faḍl.

---

# Example Discussion

Ask:

How many times has Allah:
- hidden your sins?
- forgiven you?
- protected you?
- saved you from embarrassment?
- answered your duʿā?

Yet we still fall short.

And still Allah continues giving.
